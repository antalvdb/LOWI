DOMINANT PATTERN
----------------

across several substantive sub-topics.

TEMPORAL INTERPRETATION
-----------------------
- In the earliest overlapping period (2010–2014), LOWI is distinctive mainly
  through a plagiarism-heavy cluster.
- In 2015–2019, multiple LOWI-specific appeal-procedure topics emerge, suggesting
  increasing thematic differentiation as LOWI's caseload grew.
- From 2020 onward, the English-language shift in UvNL publications becomes the
  dominant signal. This likely reflects a policy change by universities to publish
  decisions in English (possibly driven by internationalisation).
- The LOWI clinical-research topic (utrecht, metc) becoming prominent in 2020–2024
  may reflect growth in complaints involving clinical/medical research protocols.

DOMINANT PATTERN (filtered)
----------------------------
With English documents and inadmissibility decisions removed, the contrast
reduces from 12 to 8 significant topics and is now purely substantive.
The split reflects genuine procedural terminology: UvNL first-instance
decisions consistently use "beklaagde/klager" while LOWI second-instance
reviews use "verzoeker/belanghebbende". This is structurally expected —
LOWI handles appeals, not original complaints — but the G-test confirms
the difference is corpus-wide and not driven by a handful of outliers.
Topic 11 retains some English tokens, suggesting a small number of
mixed-language or code-switching documents remain after filtering.

TEMPORAL INTERPRETATION (filtered)
------------------------------------
- 2010–2014: LOWI is distinctive through a plagiarism-focused cluster, which
  may reflect the types of cases reaching LOWI in its earlier years.
- 2015–2019: LOWI-specific appeals vocabulary strongly dominates. Three
  related "verzoeker/belanghebbende" clusters cover 40–21–19% of LOWI
  documents in this period; none appear in UvNL. This likely captures the
  growth of appeal activity after the 2012 Netherlands Code of Conduct
  revision reinforced LOWI's role.
- 2020–2024: The pattern reverses — two UvNL-dominant topics appear. The
  largest (35% of UvNL) contains the token "ngwi", which is the acronym
  for the Netherlands Board on Research Integrity (Nederlandse Gedragscode
  Wetenschappelijke Integriteit), introduced in 2018 and implemented more
  widely from 2020. This suggests UvNL institutions adopted standardised
  NGWI-prescribed report templates that LOWI documents do not follow.
- 2025+: No significant differences detectable yet — the period is too
  short (17 LOWI, 9 UvNL) for the G-test to reach significance.
